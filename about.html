Who We Are

A multi-specialty practice built around how patients actually live

AAIRS Clinic & Troy Sleep Center is a Michigan-based medical practice that brings allergy, asthma, immunology, respiratory, and sleep medicine under one roof. The clinic was founded on the recognition that these specialties overlap constantly in real patients — a child with allergic rhinitis often develops asthma; an adult with poorly controlled allergies frequently snores their way into a sleep apnea diagnosis. AAIRS Clinic treats the whole picture rather than referring the patient down a hallway every time a new symptom appears.

The practice serves Troy, Sterling Heights, and the wider Oakland and Macomb County communities, with virtual telemedicine available for patients who can't make it in person. Most major insurance plans are accepted, and the team coordinates with local durable medical equipment providers, dentists, and ENT specialists to make sure each treatment plan is actually feasible — not just clinically correct on paper. Additional practice information, including the latest provider roster and office hours, is maintained on the main AAIRS Clinic website.

Sleep Medicine

An AASM-accredited sleep lab with adult and pediatric testing

The sleep program at AAIRS Clinic is the centerpiece of the practice. The Troy and Sterling Heights sleep labs are accredited by the American Academy of Sleep Medicine — an accreditation that requires ongoing audits of equipment, technician credentials, scoring accuracy, and physician oversight. The interpreting physicians at AAIRS are board certified in Sleep Medicine, which means every study is read by someone who specializes in this work, not a generalist signing off on data they only see occasionally. Patients can read more about what AASM accreditation actually means for the quality of a sleep study.

AAIRS performs both in-lab polysomnography and home sleep testing for adult patients. Home testing devices can be picked up at the clinic or shipped directly to the patient's home, which removes the most common barrier to getting tested — the logistics of spending a night somewhere that isn't your own bed. For pediatric patients, the team works exclusively with in-lab studies, where the technology and the technicians are calibrated for younger bodies. AAIRS has also been recognized as a Center of Excellence by Inspire, a designation tied to the Inspire upper airway stimulation therapy used as an alternative to CPAP for select sleep apnea patients.

Allergy & Immunology

Testing, diagnosis, and treatment for every kind of allergic disease

The allergy and immunology side of AAIRS Clinic handles the full range of allergic conditions — environmental, food, drug, insect, and contact — for patients of every age. The clinic uses skin prick testing, intradermal testing, and serum-specific IgE blood testing depending on the question being asked, and follows up with treatment plans that range from straightforward avoidance counseling to subcutaneous immunotherapy (allergy shots) and sublingual immunotherapy. Patients curious about the process can find a full walkthrough of what to expect during an allergy testing appointment.

Immunology cases — recurrent infections, suspected immunodeficiencies, and unusual inflammatory presentations — are evaluated with the same care. AAIRS Clinic has been recognized as a Top Doc by Hour Detroit on multiple occasions, a peer-nomination award that reflects how the practice is regarded by other physicians in the region. That recognition matters most when patients are looking for someone to handle a complicated case, not just a routine appointment.

Asthma & Pulmonary

Coordinated care for adult and pediatric respiratory conditions

Asthma rarely shows up alone — it tends to travel with allergies, sinus disease, reflux, and, in adults, with sleep-disordered breathing. AAIRS Clinic uses spirometry, exhaled nitric oxide testing, and detailed history-taking to figure out which asthma the patient actually has, then builds a control plan around that specific phenotype. For pediatric patients, the team works closely with parents and schools to keep daily medications, rescue inhalers, and trigger management aligned with real life. The clinic publishes regular patient education on managing asthma across the Oakland and Macomb County climate cycle.

Pulmonary cases that aren't asthma — chronic cough, suspected COPD, evaluation for shortness of breath — also fit naturally into the AAIRS workflow because the same lab equipment, the same imaging partners, and the same set of board-certified physicians can handle the evaluation without bouncing the patient between practices. When a referral is needed (interventional pulmonology, thoracic surgery), AAIRS Clinic has long-standing relationships with the appropriate Michigan specialists and routes patients accordingly.

How We Work With You

Insurance, telemedicine, and care coordination

AAIRS Clinic & Troy Sleep Center accepts most major insurance plans, including the regional carriers most commonly held in Oakland and Macomb counties. Verification happens before the first visit so patients know what their coverage looks like in advance — no surprise bills after the fact. Self-pay options exist for patients without insurance, and the front office is willing to walk through the cost of specific tests before they're scheduled.

Telemedicine is offered for consultations that don't strictly require in-person testing — medication adjustments, follow-up visits, second opinions, and intake conversations. For patients in Macomb County and the further reaches of Oakland County, that often makes the difference between getting care and skipping it. The clinic also coordinates directly with referring physicians, dentists fitting oral appliances, and DME companies delivering CPAPs, so the patient isn't left to relay messages between offices. More information on scheduling, locations, and how to reach the team is available on the contact page.
